Use Case 01
Self-Serve Reorder Portal

Start with US. Build it so UK and EU are a configuration, not a rebuild. Existing B2B customers log in, browse by collection, and place orders without touching a rep — and the same infrastructure powers every market you add after.

The Goal

Give existing commercial customers a front-end portal where they can browse products by collection (weights, cardio, benches, etc.), see their pre-negotiated B2B pricing, and reorder without a rep involved. V1 launches in the US. The architecture is set up from day one so adding UK and EU markets is additive — not a rebuild.

How to Build It
1
Enable B2B and Create Your US Market

In Shopify Admin, activate B2B under Settings → Customer accounts. Create a US Market in Markets — this becomes the container for your US B2B catalog, currency, tax rules, and domain. Every future geo (UK, EU, CA) gets its own Market added on top of this same foundation.

Markets → Add Market → United States · Currency: USD · Domain: repfitness.com/en-us
2
Build Your US B2B Catalog and Price Lists

Create a B2B-specific catalog in the Shopify admin — separate from DTC. Assign the catalog to the US Market. Set up price lists within the catalog: fixed B2B pricing or % off retail per product or collection. B2B pricing only surfaces on login — DTC visitors always see DTC pricing.

Catalogs → Create Catalog → Assign to US Market → Add Price List → Scope to collections (Weights, Cardio, Benches)
3
Create Companies and Assign to US Market

Each existing commercial account becomes a Company in Shopify. The Company record holds: contacts, locations, assigned catalog, payment terms, and order history. Companies are scoped to the US Market — when you add a UK Market later, UK companies go there. No overlap, no manual filtering.

B2B → Companies → New Company → Assign Catalog + US Market → Add Contacts + Locations
4
Set Up B2B Shipping Rules (Different from DTC)

B2B shipping profiles can be completely separate from DTC. Create a dedicated shipping profile for B2B orders: different carrier rates, freight options for large orders, and Net terms-compatible shipping. Assign the profile to your B2B catalog — DTC shipping is untouched.

Settings → Shipping → Create Profile → Assign to B2B Catalog → Set freight/LTL options for commercial orders
5
Configure Net Payment Terms

Assign Net 30 (or Net 60) terms at the Company level — each account gets the terms you negotiated. When a B2B customer checks out, the order is placed with a due date automatically set. Shopify Flow handles reminders and auto-charge when the date hits — no manual AR chasing.

Company record → Payment Terms → Net 30 / Net 60 → Shopify Flow: auto-charge trigger on due date
6
Build the Front End — Collections Browse + Quick Buy

Your theme controls the B2B portal experience. Enable collection-based browsing so commercial buyers can navigate by product type. Add a Quick Buy / Quick Order component so frequent buyers can drop quantities directly from the collection page without clicking into each PDP. V1 can use an off-the-shelf theme component — no custom dev required.

Theme editor → B2B Customer Account portal → Enable Collections nav → Add Quick Order List section (available in most Plus themes)
Scaling to UK + EU — The Markets Playbook

You build the infrastructure once. Every new geo is a configuration layer on top.

When REP is ready to open the B2B portal to UK buyers: add a UK Market, create a UK-specific price list (GBP), assign a UK catalog (same products, different pricing/VAT handling), and assign UK companies to that market. The portal front end, checkout logic, and order management are already built — you're just extending to a new lane, not rebuilding the road.

Add UK Market → GBP currency
Duplicate US catalog → Adjust UK pricing
Shopify Markets: VAT auto-applied
New UK companies → Assigned to UK Market
Same theme, same portal UX
Key Features Involved
B2B Catalogs

Scoped Product & Pricing Visibility

Each catalog controls which products a company sees and at what price. DTC visitors never see B2B pricing. Multiple catalogs can run simultaneously — US commercial, UK commercial, F45, each with different terms.

Shopify Markets

One Store, Multiple Geos

Markets controls currency, language, domain, tax rules, and which catalog applies — per geography. Adding a new country is additive. No separate Shopify store, no duplicated theme work, no split admin.

Net Payment Terms

AR Built Into the Order Flow

Net 30/60 set per company. Due dates attached to every order automatically. Shopify Flow handles dunning reminders and auto-charge — eliminating the manual AR chase and the unmatched ACH wire problem.

Use Case 02
Commercial Sign-Up Flow

A purpose-built application form for commercial buyers — collected via Shopify Forms, automatically creating an unapproved company record, and notifying the sales team in HubSpot so they can vet and activate accounts without any manual data entry.

The Goal

Commercial buyers who want a B2B account submit a form on repfitness.com. That form automatically creates a Company record in Shopify with their details, set to unapproved. The sales team sees the lead in HubSpot and either activates or declines. Once approved, the buyer gets access to the portal — no rep data entry, no email threads.

How to Build It
1
Build the Application Form with Shopify Forms

Install the Shopify Forms app (free, native). Build a custom form collecting: business name, contact name, email, phone, business type (gym, corporate, institutional), number of locations, and any commercial-specific fields REP needs (e.g., reseller cert, EIN). Embed it on a dedicated /wholesale or /commercial page.

Shopify Forms → Create Form → Custom fields → Embed on /commercial page
2
Auto-Create the Company Record on Submission

Use Shopify Flow to trigger on form submission: create a Company record in B2B with the submitted details, set status to Unapproved, and tag it with source (e.g., "Web Application"). The company exists in the system immediately — the sales team doesn't need to create it manually when they go to approve.

Shopify Flow → Trigger: Form Submitted → Action: Create Company (status: Unapproved) → Tag: Web-Application
3
Push the Lead to HubSpot

The HubSpot for Shopify integration syncs customer and company data bidirectionally. When a new unapproved company is created via Flow, a contact and deal can be created automatically in HubSpot — so the sales team's notification and pipeline management lives where they already work, not in Shopify admin.

HubSpot Shopify Integration → Map Company fields → Auto-create Contact + Deal → Trigger sales team notification
4
Sales Team Vets and Activates

The rep clicks into HubSpot, reviews the lead, and either activates the company (changing status to Approved in Shopify, assigning catalog and payment terms) or declines. Once approved, the buyer receives an invitation email to set up their account and access the portal — the whole flow is automated from form submission to active account.

Shopify Admin → B2B → Companies → Approve → Assign Catalog + Terms → Auto-send invite email
Scaling This to New Geos

Add a market-specific version of the form — same flow, different routing.

When the UK portal launches, create a /commercial-uk form variant collecting GBP and UK-specific business details. Flow routes UK submissions to UK company records (assigned to the UK Market). HubSpot gets the same notification — the sales team handles UK leads exactly as they handle US ones. No new tooling, no new processes.

Duplicate form → UK version
Flow routes to UK Market companies
Same HubSpot pipeline
UK catalog auto-assigned on approval
Key Features Involved
Shopify Forms

Native, No-Code Form Builder

Free app, native to Shopify. Builds fully customizable forms that embed anywhere on the storefront. Submissions feed directly into Shopify Flow for automation — no third-party form tool needed.

Shopify Flow

The Automation Layer

Triggers on form submission to create company records, set approval status, assign catalogs, fire HubSpot events, and send buyer invite emails — all without manual steps from the sales team.

HubSpot Integration

Leads Where the Team Already Works

The native HubSpot + Shopify integration syncs company and customer data bidirectionally. New B2B applications surface in HubSpot as deals — with all form fields mapped to the contact record.

Use Case 03
F45 Branded Market

A fully branded F45 purchasing experience — living inside REP's Shopify store — where F45 franchise owners land from F45.com, see only the F45-approved catalog at F45-negotiated pricing, and check out while REP owns every part of the transaction.

The Goal

Create a dedicated F45 market within REP's Shopify store — custom front end, F45 branding, F45-specific catalog and pricing. F45.com links franchise owners directly to the REP portal. REP owns the entire transaction: customer data, shipping, pricing, products, and checkout. F45 gets a seamless branded experience without needing to build or manage a store.

How to Build It
1
Create the F45 Market in Shopify Markets

In Shopify Markets, create a dedicated F45 market. Assign it its own subdomain or subdirectory (e.g., f45.repfitness.com or repfitness.com/f45). This market controls what products are visible, what currency applies, and what the storefront looks like — completely isolated from the standard DTC and commercial US experiences.

Markets → Add Market → Name: F45 → Domain: f45.repfitness.com · Currency: USD
2
Build the F45 Catalog and Price List

Create an F45-specific B2B catalog: only the products F45 franchise owners are eligible to purchase, at the negotiated F45 pricing tier. F45 buyers logging in will never see general commercial pricing or DTC pricing — only their catalog. Minimum order quantities, approved SKUs, and bulk pricing tiers all set within this catalog.

B2B → Catalogs → F45 Catalog → Assign F45 Price List → Scope to approved SKUs only
3
Customize the Storefront for F45 Branding

Use Shopify's theme customization to apply F45 branding to the market-specific experience — logo, colors, banner imagery, collection naming. Shopify Markets allows market-specific content sections within the same theme, so F45 buyers see an F45-branded portal while US commercial buyers see the standard REP experience — same store, no separate theme.

Theme Editor → Market-specific content blocks → F45 logo, brand colors, curated homepage sections
4
Set Up F45 Companies and Accounts

Each F45 franchise location becomes a Company (or sub-location under a master F45 account). Assign all F45 companies to the F45 market and the F45 catalog. F45 corporate can see consolidated spend across all franchisees; each location manages its own orders independently. Payment terms set per franchise or at master account level.

B2B → Companies → F45 Master Account → Locations per franchise → All assigned to F45 Market + Catalog
5
Link from F45.com — REP Owns the Transaction

F45 adds a simple "Order Equipment" link on their franchisee portal or partner page pointing to f45.repfitness.com. F45 owners land on the branded REP portal, log in (or request access), and place orders. REP controls the entire transaction: payment processing, shipping, customer data, order confirmation, and fulfillment. F45 gets a seamless experience without any commerce infrastructure of their own.

F45.com → Link to f45.repfitness.com → B2B login → F45 catalog → REP checkout + fulfillment
Why This Model Works for Other Brand Partners Too

The F45 market is a template. Every new brand partner is a new market configuration.

Once the F45 market is live, REP has a repeatable pattern for any future branded channel partnership. Another franchise brand, a corporate wellness partner, or an institutional buyer group gets their own market: branded experience, scoped catalog, dedicated pricing, and a link from their site into REP's portal. One store running unlimited partner channels — REP owns every transaction.

Shopify Markets

Isolated Partner Channel Within One Store

Markets creates a fully scoped experience — custom domain, custom content, market-specific catalog — without a separate Shopify store. F45 buyers see F45. US commercial buyers see REP commercial. Both in the same admin.

Company Hierarchies

HQ Visibility, Location-Level Ordering

F45 corporate gets a master account with consolidated reporting. Each franchise location orders independently under that account. HQ can see total spend across the network without managing every transaction.

Market-Specific Content

F45 Branding Without a Separate Theme

Shopify Markets supports market-specific content blocks in the theme editor — logo, colors, banners, featured collections. F45 owners see F45 branding. The theme is still one codebase, one admin.
